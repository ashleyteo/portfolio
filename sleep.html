Sleep Quality Analysis

The goal of the project was to examine if a causal relationship exists between an individualâ€™s sedentary minutes and their sleep quality. To start, I explored the data with a correlation table between all the variables.

From the correlation table, I decided to further analyze the two variables with a relatively high correlation and noticed that Sedentary Minutes and Total Minutes Asleep had a linear trend with a negative correlation.

Next, I checked for regression problems with a simple linear model and found irrelevant regressors, multicollinearity, and heteroscedasticity issues.

To overcome these problems, I tried an instrumental variable model and a panel regression.

						
# Panel Regression
ols <-lm(TotalMinutesAsleep ~ SedentaryMinutes, data=Fitbit2)
summary(ols)

fixed.dum <-lm(TotalMinutesAsleep ~ SedentaryMinutes + factor(Id) - 1, data=Fitbit2)
summary(fixed.dum)

fixed.IdFE<-plm(TotalMinutesAsleep~SedentaryMinutes,data=Fitbit2,index=c("Id","ActivityDate"),model="within")
summary(fixed.IdFE)

Given the available data, the panel regression showed that sleeping time reduces by 0.333 minutes, on average per individual, when sedentary minutes increased by one minute. This ties in with the FitBit goal of encouraging an active lifestyle which decreases an individual's sedentary time, leading to an increase in sleep quality.
